The Story
Where it all began
One person. One language. One gap that needed to be filled.

I built Higaad because I saw something missing. A generation raised between two languages — Somali and English — with no single place that truly honoured both. Not a rough translator. Not a relic of the past. A living, modern dictionary that respects the depth of Somali and the precision of English.

The Somali language was only officially written down in 1972 — meaning decades of oral tradition had never been properly documented in digital form. Every word added to Higaad is a small act of preservation. From everyday greetings to the language of medicine and technology, the dictionary grows to meet the world Somali speakers actually live in.

What started as a personal notebook became something much larger. Higaad is now a full dictionary, a learning platform, and a community resource — and it is entirely free. Language should never cost money. Culture should never be locked away. That is the promise this project was built on, and it will never change.
